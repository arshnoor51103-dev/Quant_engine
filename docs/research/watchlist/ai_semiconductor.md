## Why This Sector Is Interesting

**AI capex cycle:** Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) are spending at unprecedented rates on GPU clusters, custom ASICs, and datacenter infrastructure. This spending flows directly to NVDA, Broadcom, and TSMC.

**Secular growth theme:** AI inference at scale requires specialized hardware. Unlike previous tech cycles (mobile, cloud), AI compute requirements are growing faster than general-purpose GPU/CPU efficiency gains — creating structural demand for high-end semiconductors.

**Supply chain chokepoints:** ASML's EUV monopoly and TSMC's foundry dominance create structural moats. These are not easily displaced, creating durability to the growth thesis.

---

## Risks

**Concentration in NVDA:** At ~20% of CHPS.TO, a single-stock event in NVDA (regulatory action, competitive disruption, earnings miss) has outsized impact.

**Cyclical semiconductor downturns:** The semiconductor industry has historically had severe down-cycles (2001, 2008–09, 2022–23). AI capex could slow or collapse if hyperscaler ROI disappoints.

**Valuation sensitivity:** At current AI enthusiasm levels, valuations are extended. A multiple compression event (rising rates, risk-off) would hit CHPS disproportionately.

**HXQ.TO overlap:** HXQ.TO (NASDAQ-100) has NVDA as a top holding and significant tech overlap. The correlation between CHPS.TO and HXQ.TO should be monitored — if it climbs above 0.85, the diversification case weakens.
