## Why This Sector Exists in the Watchlist

The Big Six Canadian banks are among the most stable dividend-paying large-caps on the TSX. At Tier 2, individual stocks unlock — and the banks are the natural first candidates for the dividend bucket, where CDZ.TO and VDY.TO currently provide broad exposure without stock-level control.

Adding individual bank positions at Tier 2 would allow direct yield targeting and the ability to overweight the strongest dividend growers rather than holding a basket weighted by index rules.

## Why Worth Watching

**TSX concentration:** Financials are the largest sector on the TSX (~33% weight). XIC.TO already has indirect exposure, but at index weights. Individual positions allow deliberate sizing.

**Dividend bucket fit:** Canadian banks have paid and grown dividends consistently for decades, including through 2008–09 (uniquely among global banks). CDZ.TO and VDY.TO both hold all of the Big Six — adding individual names is only worth it if there's a reason to deviate from that weighting.

**Historically stable yields:** Big Six dividend yields typically range 4–6%. At Tier 2 capital levels, this becomes meaningful absolute income alongside TFSA growth.

## Current Status

Not researched. The following questions are unanswered and must be answered before a hypothesis can be created:

1. **Intra-bank correlation.** How correlated are the Big Six with each other? If RY.TO and TD.TO move in lockstep (likely), there's no diversification benefit in holding multiple individual names vs just CDZ.TO.
2. **Correlation with CDZ.TO and VDY.TO.** Are individual bank stocks mostly redundant with the existing dividend basket? If correlation > 0.85, stock selection adds noise, not signal.
3. **Which bank has the best risk-adjusted dividend growth record?** Yield alone is insufficient — payout ratio sustainability and EPS growth matter.
4. **Data history.** All Big Six have long TSX histories — data coverage is not a concern here.
